C ome apart,

Oh spring flower.

One sprung to life

From a budding clover.

Sway in the breeze un-stiff.

Sow yourself in a barren land.

Detaching you at the stems,

Wander the sky unplanned.

Planting at earth's whims,

Go together.

O h spring flower,

Look once toward the sun you know,

Keep the old field's scent linger awhile.

When the breeze comes calling, go

I'll wander somewhere with you

Bloom strange among stranger flowers,

Let the new earth know your roots.

And should you reach beneath it,

You might find mine,

Still tangled in yours.
